The History of Basketball

Basketball was invented in 1891 by Dr. James Naismith in Springfield, Massachusetts. Created as a way to keep athletes active during the winter, it quickly grew in popularity worldwide. The sport has evolved significantly over the years, becoming a global phenomenon and a staple in sports culture.

From humble beginnings with peach baskets as hoops, basketball is now a dynamic and fast-paced game played by millions. Learn how it all started and how the game has transformed over time.

Rules of Basketball

Basketball is a game of skill, strategy, and discipline, governed by a set of essential rules that ensure fair play and excitement. Here are some of the basic rules:

  • Team Composition: Each team consists of 5 players on the court at a time.
  • Game Duration: A standard game consists of four quarters, each lasting 10-12 minutes, depending on the league.
  • Scoring: Points are scored by shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop. Field goals are worth 2 or 3 points, and free throws are worth 1 point.
  • Dribbling: Players must dribble the ball while moving. Traveling occurs if they take steps without dribbling.
  • Defense: Teams must prevent their opponents from scoring without fouling.
